How to play Blackjack

Goal

Beat the dealer's hand without going over 21.

Card values

  • 2–10: face value
  • J / Q / K: 10
  • A: 1 or 11 — whichever helps your hand most

The flow

  1. You're dealt 2 cards face-up. Dealer shows 1 card; the second is hidden.
  2. You decide how to play your hand (actions below).
  3. When everyone's done, dealer flips the hidden card and draws until total is 17 or higher.
  4. If your total beats the dealer's without going over 21 — you win.

Your actions

  • Hit — Take another card. If you go over 21, you bust and lose.
  • Stand — Stop drawing. Lock in your total.
  • Double — Double your bet, then take exactly one more card and stand. Big risk, big payoff. Only on your first 2 cards.
  • Split — If your first two cards have the same rank (e.g. two 8s, or a 10 and a Jack), split them into two separate hands, each with its own bet. You play each hand in turn.
  • Surrender — Give up the hand. You lose half your bet but keep the other half. Only on your first 2 cards.
  • Insurance — Offered only when the dealer's up-card is an Ace. Costs half your bet. Pays 2:1 if the dealer has Blackjack — otherwise you lose it.

Payouts

  • Win: 1× your bet
  • Blackjack (Ace + 10/face on first 2 cards): 1.5× your bet
  • Push (tie): bet returned
  • Bust / Lose: bet lost

House rules at this table

  • 6-deck shoe
  • Dealer stands on all 17 (including soft 17)
  • Blackjack pays 3:2
  • You can split up to 4 hands
  • Split aces get one card only and don't count as Blackjack
  • Each round opens for 20 seconds — bet during that window or sit out
  • Minimum bet: 50 chips. If you drop below 50, you're out
